Our Team

Recovery Grads was founded in January 2012, by Holly Sherman and Sharon
Weber, whose team of compassionate collaborators bring a diverse range
of expertise from the following fields: recovery/addiction treatment, law, marketing,
medicine, training & instructional design, creative arts, strategy and sales. The
team is comprised of many members in long-term recovery whose experience
inspires their work with empathy, hope and respect for those affected by addiction.

Holly Sherman, B.A., LL.B., J.D.
Co-founder

Holly brings a diverse background in
psychology and law to Recovery Grads
with an undergraduate degree from
Vassar and law degrees from The
London School of Economics and the
University of Texas. After several years
working as a journalist and mediator,
she served as Program Manager and
Marketing Director of Herbert House
Sober Living in Southern California for
2 years and has free-lanced as a
marketing and program consultant for
several Los Angeles sober livings. Holly
is “out” in her declaration of gratitude
for her long-term recovery.

Sharon Weber, B.A., M.B.A.
Co-founder

Sharon is a passionate consumer and
brand champion with more than 35
years of marketing and advertising
experience and a strong background in
business strategy. She has launched
global and national brands for
communications agencies in New York,
Los Angeles and Tokyo, including J.
Walter Thompson, SSC&B:Lintas
Worldwide and Jack Morton
Worldwide. In long-term recovery
herself, she is combining her passion
for recovery education and advocacy
with her branding and marketing
expertise to launch The Haven. Sharon
serves as a Regional Alumni Volunteer
for The Betty Ford Center.

Elise Rovens, B. A., M. A., MFT
Partner

Elise brings a wealth of work and life
experience to the Recovery Grads team
across a broad range of multiple
disciplines of clinical work,
marketing & sales, real estate
and fundraising. She is a licensed MFT with
a Masters Degree in Clinical psychology
from Antioch University and a
Bachelors Degree in Economics from
UCLA and has worked with students,
parents and adolescents on a diverse
range of issues but specializes in
several areas of trauma, sexual &
physical abuse and eating disorders
in addition to various types of addiction.
Elise has completed several courses in
mindfulness training and is certified in
Neurofeedback, both helpful in clinical
work as well as her own personal recovery.
Elise serves as a "dot connector" for
Recovery Grads, connecting previously
unrelated people, concepts and
ideas for the benefit of The Haven
communities and students.

Chris Hart, B.A.
Community Strategist

Chris has made a career out of working
with organizations interested in
building community capacities for
healthy and sustainable change—first
as a leader within organizations and
then through his consulting firm based
in Chicago. He is the creator and chief
interpreter of, “The Path to Sustainable
Change”, a model that is helping
Recovery Grads build the recovery
community in each of The Havens.
Chris brings a wealth of experience

Nick Techentin, Ph.D
The Haven USC Program Director

“Dr. Nick” is a clinical psychologist
specializing in dual-diagnosis
treatment. He holds a doctorate in
psychology and has been program
director of an exclusive Malibu rehab.
An alum of USC, Dr. Nick has extensive
experience with co-occurring disorders
that affect many college students like
depression, anxiety, mood disorders,
pain disorders, personality disorders
and trauma. Dr. Nick was trained,
post-doctorate, in the neurobiology
and holistic treatment of substance
abuse. He brings with him the respect
and professionalism every student
seeking long-term recovery deserves.

Nancy Marcus Newman, Esq.
President, The Bridge Foundation
Parent and Community Liaison

Nancy Marcus Newman is an attorney,
child and youth welfare advocate, and
nonprofit consultant in the
Philadelphia area. She is a person in
long-term recovery, Founder of The
Bridge Foundation, which supports

families, and has been a tireless
advocate for legislative and systems
reform supporting youth. She is a

Pennsylvania Law School and is a

DC Bars. Prior to pursuing public

Leslie Reynolds-Rottman, MFT
Treatment Professional,
